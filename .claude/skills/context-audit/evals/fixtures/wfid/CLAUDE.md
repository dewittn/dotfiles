# CLAUDE.md - Waking From Innocent Dreams

**Waking From Innocent Dreams (WFID)** is a Pensieve — an insight discovery space for examining Nelson's novel manuscript through AI-assisted dialogue. It evolves through conversation as new insights emerge and patterns become clear.

## CRITICAL: Start Here

**ALWAYS read these files first** before any task in this Pensieve:

1. **`working-with-nelson.md`** — Universal guidance on communication style, thinking patterns, and collaboration approach.

2. **`key-insights-wfid.md`** — Domain-specific insights, patterns, and discoveries for WFID.

## Purpose & Goals

This workspace supports the ongoing development of "Waking From Innocent Dreams," a novel about a teenage adoptee who discovers through a DNA test that he was forcibly disappeared as an infant during the Salvadoran Civil War.

**Current phase:** Manuscript V2 — incorporating magical realism and historical fiction elements into the story structure.

**Goals:**

- Develop craft-level feedback on structure, character arcs, and magical realism implementation
- Maintain a tamper-evident audit trail of all AI conversations (see `audit-log/`)
- Build cross-session knowledge so the professor and other agents can give informed feedback

## Document Authority Notes

- **Story Summary** (`Structure/WAKING FROM INNOCENT DREAMS - Story Summary.md`) is the canonical source for plot, character arcs, and structure. If other documents contradict it, the summary is authoritative (it's the most recent synthesis).
- **Beat Sheet** (`Structure/Save The Cat Beat Sheet - Story Outline.md`) provides detailed scene-level structure. Cross-reference with Story Summary for any discrepancies.
- **MOC WFID.md** reflects the actual files in this repository. Keep it updated when files are added, moved, or removed.
- **Inbox items** are recent notes that haven't been triaged — they may contain the freshest thinking. Triage at startup.

## Agent Behavior Guidelines

**For detailed guidance, see `working-with-nelson.md`.**

Quick reference:

1. **Be direct, evidence-based.** Data over encouragement.
2. **Update insights proactively.** Propose additions to `key-insights-wfid.md` when discoveries emerge.
3. **Use Nelson's frameworks.** Reference concepts he's articulated.

### WFID-Specific Guidelines

- **Don't rewrite Nelson's prose.** This is his story in his voice. Provide craft feedback, not rewrites.
- **Use the document inventory.** When discussing a character, theme, or structural element, proactively read the relevant files rather than making assumptions.
- **Reference the beat sheet.** Structural feedback should connect to the Save The Cat framework Nelson uses.
- **Respect the magical realism framework.** Magic in WFID follows specific rules (see key-insights). Don't suggest magical elements that violate these principles.
- **The ghost is central.** Any discussion of magical realism should consider the ghost's role and behavior.
- **Don't read `Chats/` files for session context.** Use `search_professor_feedback` and snapshots instead — `Chats/` files are Obsidian-formatted exports of the same data.
